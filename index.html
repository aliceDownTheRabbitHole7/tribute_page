Stan Lee

A hero to our imaginations

1922 - 2018

Early Career

In 1939, Stan Lee started working as an assisstant at Timely Comics, which would later become Marvel Comics. The genre was pioneered by DC Comics at the time with heroes such as Superman, Batman, and Wonder Woman. Lee worked for Timely as a text filler for comics including Captain America, the Submariner, the Human Torch which would eventually evolve into the Fantastic Four. He even wrote for the company while serving in the US Army in World War II, sending in his work by mail.

Post War

After the war subsided, so did the popularity of comic book superheroes. DC comics had revitalized the genre again by creating the new superhero The Flash. In order to compete in the again rising popularity, Stan Lee had helped to create The Fantastic Four with Jack Kirby in 1961. The upsurge in popularity caused the creation of many popular heroes today, including Spider-man.

Extraordinary Human

Stan Lee brought a more relateable side to heroes that hadn't been seen from Marvel's counterpart, DC Comics. He gave heroes real world problems. Spider-man was a highschool nerd as compared to Superman who was considered a god amongst people. The X-Men suffered prejudice and exile by humans.

A hero to us all

Many of Stan Lee's creations would help mold the imaginations of people young and old for decades to come, eventually becoming the household name that it is known as today. Stan Lee has become a person that we all hold in our memories.
